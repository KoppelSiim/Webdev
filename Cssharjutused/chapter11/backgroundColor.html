The composition of seawater

Seawater, or salt water, is water from a sea or ocean.

On average, seawater in the world's oceans has a salinity of about 3.5% (35 g/L, 35 ppt, 600 mM).
"This means that every kilogram (roughly one liter by volume) of seawater has approximately 35 grams (1.2 oz) of dissolved salts (predominantly sodium (Na+) and chloride (Cl−) ions). The average density at the surface is 1.025 kg/L. Seawater is denser than both fresh water and pure water (density 1.0 kg/L at 4 °C (39 °F)) because the dissolved salts increase the mass by a larger proportion than the volume.
